About the Waukesha Condominium Consortium

The Waukesha Condominium Consortium is a group of condominium associations working together to improve relationships with the City of Waukesha and share best practices among members.

The Consortium is not a legal entity and does not provide legal advice. Prospective owners should review association documents and consult legal or real estate professionals before purchasing.

Purpose of This Guide

This guide provides information to help prospective condominium buyers understand responsibilities, fees, governance, and lifestyle differences.

Condominium Living vs Single-Family Homes

Single-family homes generally follow city requirements for public streets, sidewalks, and setbacks. Homeowners are responsible for all maintenance.

Condominium developments typically follow Planned Unit Development rules, allowing private roads and shared responsibility among owners.

Planned Unit Developments (PUDs)

Developers must submit a PUD to the City of Waukesha outlining building plans and association governance. Since PUDs often use private infrastructure, city services such as snow plowing and trash pickup are not provided.

Why Condominiums Have an Association

Associations govern the condominium, managing contractors, maintenance, rules, and long‑term planning. Control typically transitions from the developer to owners as units are sold.

Fees and Reserve Funds

Monthly association fees pay for maintenance, trash services, snow removal, lawn care, and building upkeep. Reserve funds cover major future expenses, and special assessments may be required.

Implications for Owners

Owners are responsible for everything “from the walls in.” Associations insure exterior structures, while owners typically carry HO‑6 insurance for personal property and interior improvements.

Because exterior areas are shared, association rules apply to landscaping, signage, and modifications.
